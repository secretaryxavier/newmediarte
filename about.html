About This Project

Why This Site Exists

This project uses the visual language of hate speech websites as a Trojan Horse to teach about their mechanisms. By mimicking the aesthetics of extremist sites, we aim to:

  • Expose the cognitive, affective, and algorithmic patterns that underlie harmful communication
  • Create a space for critical literacy about harmful rhetoric
  • Provide tools for recognizing and deconstructing manipulative communication

Ethical Considerations

While the site uses the visual language of hate speech, it:

  • Includes clear warnings and disclaimers
  • Avoids actual hate speech imagery or content
  • Uses satire and inversion techniques to critique harmful patterns
  • Provides educational annotations and explanations

How to Use This Site

Explore the different sections to learn about:

  • Mechanisms - The cognitive, affective, and algorithmic patterns of hate speech
  • Examples - Redacted and annotated real-world cases
  • Blog - Satirical posts with critical analysis
