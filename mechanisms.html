Understanding the Mechanisms

Cognitive Mechanisms

▼

Key Patterns:

  • Confirmation Bias
  • Out-group Dehumanization
  • Moral Superiority
  • Projection

These cognitive processes create and reinforce harmful worldviews by selectively interpreting information and dehumanizing others.

Affective Mechanisms

▼

Emotional Strategies:

  • Moral Panic
  • Outrage Amplification
  • Fear-mongering
  • Emotional Contagion

Hate speech often uses emotional manipulation to create a sense of urgency and moral outrage, driving engagement and amplification.

Algorithmic Mechanisms

▼

Platform Dynamics:

  • Engagement Algorithms
  • Echo Chambers
  • Content Amplification
  • Recommendation Systems

Online platforms can unintentionally amplify harmful content through their design and algorithms, creating feedback loops of radicalization.

Rhetorical Mechanisms

▼

Language Patterns:

  • In-group vs. Out-group Language
  • Loaded Language
  • False Dilemmas
  • Slippery Slope Arguments

Hate speech often uses specific rhetorical techniques to create divisiveness and manipulate perception.
